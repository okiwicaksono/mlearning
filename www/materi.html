Fact VS Opinion

A fact is a specific detail that is true based on objective proof.
An opinion is an interpretation, value judgment, or belief that cannot be proved or disproved. An opinion is created.

For example, look at the picture!
Fact : Floods hit the town. (reality, based on photo)
Opinion : Many people don't care about their town. (not real, only people's opinion)

What is an opinion?

An opinion is the way you feel or think about something. Our opinion about something or someone is based on our perspective. Whenever we give or express our opinion it is important to give reasoning or an example to support our opinion.

Indicating Opinion

Here are some words and phrases that can help you indicate an opinion:

I think...
In my opinion...
I feel...
to my mind...
I guess...
ect.

Statement
Student A: I don't like playing tag because people end up fighting.
Student B: In my opinion, the government should provide means of sustenance for under privileged people instead of building tall towers.

# Student A give a statement and Student B give an opinion about that statement.

Suggestions

There are some words or phrases that can help you indicate a suggestion:

You should...
You could...
ough to...
Why don't...?
I wish...
Shall I...
Let's...
ect.

For Example:
  • You could ask Mrs Linda to help you with your English lessons. Or I could try to help you.
  • Why don't you come around seven

Expressions

Opinions can be expressed in the ways given below:

  • Personal Point of View

    These expressions are used to show personal point of view.

    You can use this phrases:

    In my opinion...
    I think...
    Personally, I think...
    According to me...
    As I understand...
    What I mean is...
    To my mind...
    As I see it...
    ect.

    For examples:

    • In my opinion, big companies should pay less tax.
    • I think Yogyakarta is developing fast.

  • General Point of view

    These expressions are used to show general (some peoples) point of view. General point of view creates a balace in writing and helps to avoid absolute statements.

    You can use this phrases:

    Generally it is accepted...
    Majority agree with...
    It is considered...
    Most people don't agree...
    Almost everyone...
    Some people believe...
    ect.

    You can agree or disagree as long as you have reason for you opinion.
  • Agreeing

    These are some of the expressions used to express agreement with an opinion.

    You can use this phrases:

    Of course.
    I agree. I never thought of that.
    Neither do I.
    That's a good point.
    I think so too.
    I agree with this opinion.
    I couldn't agree more.
    ect.

  • Disagreeing

    These are the expressions used to express disagreement with an opinion.

    You can use this phrases:

    I disagree with you.
    I think you are wrong.
    I am sorry, I don't agree with you.
    I do not believe that.
    By this I mean...
    ect.

    For examples:
    Statement:
       Using handphone should be banned in the schools.
    Agree:
       I totally agree that using handphone should be banned in school.
    Disagree:
       I don't agree with you. Handphone is usful for student to develop their language skills.

    Opinions can be expressed using mental verbs like believe, think, doubt, assume, feel, ect.

Tag Question

Tag question (also known as tail question) is a grammatical structure in which a declarative statement or an imperative is turned into a question by adding an interrogative fragment (the "tag").

For example:
  • Televisions should show more educational programs, shouldn't they?
  • There are a lot of crimes in cities, aren't there?
  • Rubbish makes the city ugly, doesn't it?
  • School fee is really expensive, isn'it?
  • Many people don't care about our town, do they?
  • You can't have a free schooling, can you?
  • Edcatio isn't cheap, is it?
  • There are not enough public parks in this town, are there?
  • She can hardly see without her glasses, can't she?
